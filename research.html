Research & Publications

My research focuses on stability and contingency-sensitive control of low-inertia power systems, with emphasis on grid-forming inverters and hybrid grids. I work on optimization- and AI-based methods for contingency detection and adaptive control, with validation using Real-Time Digital Simulator (RTDS).

Optimal Adaptive Droop Control with AI-Based Contingency Detection for Frequency Regulation in Low-Inertia Power Systems

Manuscript in preparation — planned submission

Status: In progress · Planned submission

Develops an AI-assisted adaptive droop control strategy for hybrid low-inertia grids (synchronous generators + IBRs) on the IEEE 39-bus benchmark, enabling fast contingency detection and optimal droop gain allocation with RTDS-based validation.

An Optimal Adaptive Control for VSM-Based Grid-Forming Inverters

Prepared for submission — submission planned to IEEE EDM 2026

Status: Prepared for submission · Planned: IEEE EDM 2026

Proposes an adaptive droop control framework for VSM-based grid-forming inverters using a virtual governor to deliver optimal frequency support under credible contingencies (e.g., generator or inverter disconnection). Validated via HIL testing using RTDS NovaCor and dSPACE MicroLabBox-II with real-time GTAI/GTAO signal exchange.
