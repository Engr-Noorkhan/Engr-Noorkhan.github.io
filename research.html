Research & Publications

My research focuses on stability and contingency-sensitive control of low-inertia power systems, with emphasis on grid-forming inverters and hybrid grids. I work on optimization- and AI-based methods for contingency detection and adaptive control, with validation using Real-Time Digital Simulator (RTDS).

Optimal Adaptive Droop Control with AI-Based Contingency Detection for Frequency Regulation in Low-Inertia Power Systems

N. Khan, O. O. Khamisov, S. Akhtar, M. A. Anwar, and P. Wongsuwan

Manuscript in preparation â€” final validation and writing stage

Status: In progress Â· Final stage Â· Near submission

This work develops an AI-assisted adaptive droop control framework for hybrid power systems using an IEEE 39-bus benchmark with both synchronous generators and inverter-based resources (IBRs). A data-driven AI model enables fast contingency detection and triggers optimal droop gain allocation for enhanced frequency stability. The proposed approach is validated through RTDS-based real-time simulation.
