Role: Power System Protection & Automation Intern  |  Organization: Tekvel, Moscow, Russia  |  Duration: Jun 2025 -- Jul 2025

During my industrial immersion at Tekvel, a company specializing in power system protection and automation, I worked on the development and validation of a differential relay testing framework based on IEC 61850 digital substations. The work focused on enabling accurate, repeatable, and hardware-efficient relay testing by replacing conventional current transformer (CT) hardware with software-generated Sampled Values (SV).

Key Responsibilities & Contributions

  • Designed and validated differential protection test scenarios covering healthy operation, internal winding faults, and external through-fault conditions.
  • Developed a Python-based IEC 61850 Sampled Value (SV) generator to emulate CT measurements and stream real-time data into Tekvel Magic.
  • Performed end-to-end digital relay testing, including SV injection and GOOSE-based trip signal monitoring.
  • Evaluated relay operating time, selectivity, and dependability, ensuring fast fault detection with no false trips under normal or external-fault conditions.

Results & Outcomes

  • Reliable relay operation for internal faults with ~12 ms response time
  • No false trips during healthy-load and external-fault scenarios
  • Reduced relay testing time and dependence on physical test hardware
  • Validated a complete IEC 61850 SV & GOOSE-based testing workflow
  • Awarded Best Industrial Immersion Project 2025 for technical merit and implementation quality
